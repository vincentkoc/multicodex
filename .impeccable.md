## Design Context

### Users

Small hackathon teams at an OpenAI event. They need to choose a feasible idea,
split work, communicate, use one Codex workspace per person, resolve dependency
conflicts, and present a coherent result under time pressure.

### Brand Personality

Playful, sharp, collaborative. MultiCodex should feel like a lively studio
control room run by a calm, useful facilitator.

### Aesthetic Direction

Desktop-first light workbench with ink-like text, paper-white working surfaces,
strong red, green, blue, and yellow role signals, compact controls, and a
visible shared timeline. Avoid dark neon developer dashboards, purple
gradients, oversized marketing type, floating card grids, and generic chat UI.

### Design Principles

1. Keep the team, current work, and room conversation visible together.
2. Make conductor interventions visible, attributable, and easy to inspect.
3. Use color to distinguish roles and state, never as decoration alone.
4. Optimize for fast scanning and repeated action under a short event timer.
5. Let the live project preview become the visual payoff.
